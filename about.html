ABOUT ME

As a PhD Candidate/Teaching Assistant at University of Alabama, I teach, supervise labs, and conduct research related to GIS, Remote Sensing and Fluvial Geomorphology. Previously, I served as a Lecturer at Kathmandu University, where I taught, supervised undergraduate and graduate students of Geomatics, Civil and Environmental Engineering.

In March 2017 and Sept 2013, I graduated from University of Muenster receiving a M.S. in Geoinformatics and B.E. in Geomatics Engineering, respectively. While pursuing my undergraduate and graduate degrees, I worked as a GIS and Remote Sensing Trainee at University of Twente, International Training Center (ITC). This included working on a land cover and climate change prediction, adaptation project in partnership with ERASMUS student exhange for developing web maps and mobile application for rugged terrain using free and open source platforms (FOSS).

I hope to continue my career in Remote sensing, GIS, Fluvial Geomorphology and Earth science. I am also passionate about doing research relating to Fluvial systems, Machine Learning, Flood risks, terrestrial and coastal ecosystems, atmospheric science, and the threats we face by climate change. My goal is to increase skills in field surveying, programming, and utilize geospatial technology to better understand our earth and environment.
