ABOUT ME

About Me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate and Teaching Assistant at the University of Alabama, specializing in GIS, Remote Sensing, and Fluvial Geomorphology. My academic and professional journey reflects a deep commitment to advancing geospatial science and applying cutting-edge technologies to solve complex environmental challenges.

Previously, I served as a Lecturer at Kathmandu University, where I taught and mentored undergraduate and graduate students in Geomatics, Civil, and Environmental Engineering, fostering innovation and practical skills in geospatial technologies.

I hold a Master of Science in Geoinformatics (2017) from the University of Muenster and a Bachelor of Engineering in Geomatics Engineering (2013). During my studies, I gained international experience as a GIS and Remote Sensing Trainee at the University of Twente (ITC), contributing to projects on land cover change, climate adaptation, and web/mobile GIS solutions for rugged terrains using Free and Open Source Software (FOSS) under the ERASMUS exchange program.

My research focuses on river systems, machine learning, flood risk assessment, and climate change impacts. I am passionate about leveraging geospatial technologies, data science, and field-based approaches to better understand Earth processes and develop solutions for sustainable environmental management.
